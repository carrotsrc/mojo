From the basics of abstraction

Perspective of operational architecture

  • CPU operations can be thought of as nodes, or operation nodes

  • A routine in assembly is very much like a chain of operation nodes. A routine can be accessed via it's interconnectable start node. Higher level concepts such as loops are in themselves routines with edges that feedback to the start node of the routine.

  • An application is a subgraph, a module within a much larger network of interactions. The nature of an application is a cluster of highly intra-connected nodes of routines, each made up of nodes of operations.
